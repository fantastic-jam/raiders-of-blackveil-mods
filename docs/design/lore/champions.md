# Raiders of Blackveil — Champions

> Sources: Game Rant interviews, Steam store page, PCGamesN reveal, Steam community discussions (Jamera thread, balance thread), NotebookCheck coverage, DLCompare EA launch article.
>
> Note: As of Early Access (December 2025), four champions are available. Four more are confirmed planned but not yet released. Ability names and lore backgrounds are sparse in official sources — where mechanical detail is noted it comes from community discussions rather than official documentation.

---

## Overview

Champions are the playable rebels of the Liberator. Each is a distinct anthropomorphic animal character with a defined role, visual identity, and set of unique abilities. The design philosophy is explicitly MOBA-inspired: each champion has a strong identity, a primary role in the team (tank, damage dealer, support), and abilities that create different playstyles rather than just stat differences.

Eight **class archetypes** exist as a perk/build layer on top of champions: **Mage, Assassin, Priest, Guardian, Monk, Druid, Warrior, Warlock.** Over 300 perks are distributed across these classes. Champions are not locked to a class — the class system is the build layer, not the character definition. Special **Duo Perks** unlock when teammates combine complementary classes, rewarding coordinated team composition.

## Playable Champions (Early Access Launch Roster)

### Ironhorn

**Species:** Rhinoceros (male)  
**Role:** Tank / Frontline  
**Class archetype affinity:** Warrior / Guardian

The "armor-clad rhinoceros," Ironhorn is the rebellion's frontline bruiser. He is built for durability and close-range control. His defensive ability is confirmed to **block incoming projectiles**, making him effective at shielding teammates as well as himself.

Known ability notes (community-sourced):
- Has a dedicated **Defense** ability capable of intercepting projectile attacks.
- Fulfils the classic tank fantasy — high durability, frontline presence.

*Lore background: Not detailed in available public sources as of this writing.*

### Beatrice

**Species:** Bumblebee (female)  
**Role:** Support / Druid  
**Class archetype affinity:** Druid / Priest

Beatrice is the bumblebee druid — the rebellion's primary support champion. Her kit centres on nature magic, summons, and ally empowerment:

- **Root summons**: she calls roots from the ground to control enemy movement.
- **Seed mechanic**: she spawns seeds that can be "awakened" and transformed into **turrets**, giving her a persistent damage/area-denial presence.
- **Healing**: she can heal allies and provide them with **armor buffs** and other stat enhancements.

Beatrice is the only support-oriented champion in the launch roster, making her a natural team anchor in co-op runs.

*Lore background: Not detailed in available public sources as of this writing.*

---

### Jamera

**Species:** Cheetah (female)  
**Role:** Ranged DPS / Mage  
**Class archetype affinity:** Mage

Jamera is the fire mage of the rebellion — a glass-cannon ranged damage dealer. The developers describe her as "very agile," jumping around the battlefield, burning enemies, and making them explode. She has the **longest range** of any launch champion.

Known abilities (community-sourced):
- **Primary attack**: ranged projectiles at long range.
- **Offense ability**: a fire-based attack with a notable casting delay.
- **Defensive ability**: a pushback that repels both enemies and Jamera herself (repositioning tool).
- **Special ability (Sun Strike / Fiery State)**: creates a **ring of fire** applying a burning status effect; grants **+25% crit chance** while active.
- **Ultimate**: a mobility/AOE damage ability; reportedly has a cooldown reset mechanic on kills (functionality disputed in community discussions).
- **Dashes**: multiple mobility options, used for i-frame dodging.

Jamera is described in community discussions as a "glass cannon" — critically lower health than other champions, but exceptional damage output at range. Her burning status effects are noted as scaling less effectively in later loops.

*Lore background: Not detailed in available public sources as of this writing.*

### Draksyl

**Species:** Chameleon (male)  
**Role:** Melee DPS / Assassin  
**Class archetype affinity:** Assassin

Draksyl is the chameleon assassin — a melee-range burst damage dealer who contrasts with Jamera's ranged approach. His kit includes self-scaling passive abilities and an ultimate that deals significant single-target damage.

Known abilities (community-sourced):
- **Q ability**: applies a **stun/mark** to enemies.
- **Passive**: self-scaling, rewards consistent engagement.
- **Ultimate**: massive damage output, reported to nearly one-shot bosses in early loops; accounts for a high proportion of total damage even when building purely for attack damage.
- **Melee attacks**: hit multiple enemies simultaneously, providing natural AoE that procs on-hit effects across groups.

Community notes characterise Draksyl as mechanically powerful, particularly against bosses, with his AoE melee being important for triggering proc-based perk effects.

*Lore background: Not detailed in available public sources as of this writing.*

## Design Notes

The developers have drawn explicit comparisons to League of Legends in terms of champion design depth — distinct personalities, distinct playstyles, and a roster intended to grow over time. Each champion is intended to offer a meaningfully different experience rather than variations on the same archetype. The MOBA influence is visible in how champions have defined roles (tank/damage/support) that interact with the co-op structure, while the roguelite perk system adds a build layer on top of that fixed identity.
