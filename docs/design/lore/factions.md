# Raiders of Blackveil — Factions

> Sources: Game Rant interviews, Steam store page, PCGamesN reveal, Worthplaying/DLCompare coverage.

---

## The Blackveil Corporation

**Type:** Antagonist faction — human mega-corporation  
**Role in the world:** Dominant industrial power of Wildguard

Blackveil is the defining force of oppression in the world of Wildguard. Run and staffed by humans, it operates as an industrial mega-corporation that has commodified nature itself. It enslaves anthropomorphic animals as labour, mass-produces low-quality goods, and enforces what the developers call "a mindless consumer society" across the world.

Blackveil's operations are physically represented by massive factory complexes — dirty, industrial, full of conveyor belts — that players raid each session. The corporation responds to intrusions with escalating security forces ("rushing forces" that lock down the facility on an alarm timer).

### Known Blackveil Operations

| Operation Site | Description |
|---|---|
| **Meat Factory** | The first and primary operation site at Early Access launch. Blackveil creates low-quality industrial food here for the world of Wildguard. Described as "a dark, nasty, very dirty place with a lot of conveyor belts." |
| **Harvest Operation** | A second biome outside the Meat Factory. Features 11 new enemy types, new environmental mechanics, mini-boss encounters, and a final boss built to challenge experienced raiders. Named bosses confirmed: **Candle Amalgamation** and **Apex Sapling**. |

### Leadership

A named CEO of Blackveil is referenced as the ultimate villain of the game's campaign. As of Early Access launch, this character has not been revealed or named publicly. Defeating them is stated as the rebellion's long-term objective.

### Enemy Forces

Blackveil deploys a variety of enemies inside its facilities. Confirmed types (partial list from community sources):
- Snipers
- Bandit-type enemies
- "Otis" — a named boss character with abilities including **Smash**, **Meat Delivery**, and **Puke Sweep**
- Mini-bosses per biome
- Enemy modifiers: **Regenerators**, **Mortars**, **Fortified**, and others

## The Rebellion (The Liberator)

**Type:** Player faction — animal resistance movement  
**Role in the world:** Guerrilla resistance against Blackveil

The Rebellion is the faction players belong to. It is an organised movement of anthropomorphic animal citizens who refuse to accept Blackveil's domination. The rebels conduct surgical strikes against Blackveil installations to:

1. Steal resources and loot to fund the rebellion
2. Sabotage and destroy Blackveil manufacturing
3. Free prisoners held in corporate facilities
4. Expose and undermine Blackveil's exploitation of animals and nature

### The Liberator

The Rebellion's home base is an **airship called the Liberator**. This is the player hub between raids — players return here after each run to interact with fellow rebels, upgrade perks, and plan the next operation. The Liberator serves both as a narrative anchor (the resistance has a home, a community) and as a mechanical hub (upgrades, perk selection).

### Composition

The rebellion is made up of anthropomorphic animal champions and their allies. As of Early Access, four named champions represent the playable core of the faction (see `champions.md`). Four additional champions are planned for future updates.

### Narrative Standing

The rebellion is explicitly framed as an underdog force. Players go in lightly-armed, improvise their builds from whatever loot they find inside the factory, and must escape before lockdown — the extraction failure state (losing loot) mirrors the reality that the rebellion cannot sustain losses indefinitely. Every successful raid strengthens the cause; every failed extraction is a setback.
